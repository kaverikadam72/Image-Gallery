Types of Classical Dances

Bharatanatyam

Originating from Tamil Nadu, Bharatanatyam is one of the oldest classical dance forms, known for its graceful movements, intricate footwork, and expressive storytelling through hand gestures (mudras) and facial expressions. It was traditionally performed in temples as a form of devotion.

Kathak

Hailing from North India, Kathak is a dynamic dance form known for its fast spins, rhythmic footwork, and expressive storytelling. Influenced by both Hindu temple traditions and Mughal court performances, it blends grace with powerful movements and intricate musical compositions.

Kuchipudi

Originating from Andhra Pradesh, Kuchipudi combines fluid dance movements with dramatic storytelling, often featuring expressive facial gestures and lively footwork. Originally performed by male dancers in temples, it has evolved into a widely celebrated dance form performed by both men and women.

Odissi

Originating in Odisha, Odissi is one of the most lyrical and fluid classical dances, inspired by ancient temple sculptures. Characterized by graceful postures, intricate hand gestures, and expressive storytelling, it portrays themes from Hindu mythology, particularly Lord Jagannath.

Mohiniyattam

A graceful and feminine dance form from Kerala, Mohiniyattam is characterized by slow, swaying movements, delicate expressions, and soft footwork. Traditionally performed by women, it focuses on themes of love and devotion, with influences from both Bharatanatyam and Kathakali.

Kathakali

A dramatic and visually striking dance-theater from Kerala, Kathakali is known for its elaborate costumes, bold facial makeup, and powerful expressions. Performers use precise gestures, eye movements, and body postures to narrate mythological stories with intense emotion.

Sattriya

A sacred dance-drama from Assam, Sattriya was traditionally performed in monasteries as a form of spiritual storytelling. It blends powerful footwork with elegant hand movements, depicting scenes from Hindu epics while maintaining a strong devotional essence.

Manipuri

A deeply spiritual and delicate dance from Manipur, Manipuri is known for its soft, flowing movements, circular patterns, and devotional themes. Often depicting stories of Lord Krishna and Radha, it emphasizes grace and serenity over sharp, rhythmic footwork.

Dance Attire

Bharatanatyam(Female)Bharatanatyam – Women wear a richly pleated saree stitched for easy movement, adorned with temple jewelry, flowers, and ghungroos (ankle bells).
Bharatanatyam(Male)Bharatanatyam -Men wear a dhoti with a decorative waistband and angavastram (drape) for elegance.
Kathak(Female)Kathak – Women wear a long, flowing Anarkali dress with a churidar (tight-fitting leggings) and a dupatta, often with intricate embroidery.
Kathak(Male)Kathak-Men wear a simple kurta with a churidar or dhoti, sometimes with a waistcoat for added grace.
Kuchipudi(Female)Kuchipudi – Women wear a saree similar to Bharatanatyam but often lighter, with a pleated fan-like extension.
Kuchipudi(Male)Kuchipudi –Men wear a dhoti and angavastram, allowing flexibility for both graceful and dramatic movements.
Odissi(Female) Odissi- Women wear a silk saree with silver filigree jewelry, a unique waistband, and a headpiece resembling temple sculptures.
Odissi(Male)Odissi-Men wear a dhoti with a traditional belt and an upper cloth draped around the torso.
Mohiniyattam(Female)Mohiniyattam – Women wear an elegant white and gold kasavu saree, pleated in the front for easy movement, with minimal yet graceful jewelry.
Kathakali(Female)Kathakali – Both men and women wear elaborate, layered skirts with heavily embroidered jackets and striking face makeup. A distinctive headgear and exaggerated facial colors symbolize different characters.
Kathakali(Male)Kathakali – Both men and women wear elaborate, layered skirts with heavily embroidered jackets and striking face makeup. A distinctive headgear and exaggerated facial colors symbolize different characters.
Sattriya(Female)Sattriya – Women wear the traditional Assamese mekhela chador in bright colors, accessorized with Assamese jewelry.
Sattria(Male)Sattriya- Men wear a white dhoti, kurta, and a traditional turban, reflecting the dance’s monastic roots.
Manipuri(Female)Manipuri – Women wear a cylindrical skirt (potloi) with delicate veils and jewelry, creating an ethereal look
Manipuri(Male)Manipuri – Men wear a simple dhoti with a kurta and a traditional turban, keeping the attire modest yet elegant.

Famous Dancers

Rukmini Devi was a key figure in reviving Bharatanatyam. She modernized the dance while preserving its traditions. She founded **Kalakshetra**, a premier dance school in India. Her contributions helped Bharatanatyam gain global recognition.
Birju Maharaj was a legendary Kathak dancer. He belonged to the Lucknow Gharana of Kathak. His graceful movements and storytelling were iconic. He was a recipient of the Padma Vibhushan award.
Sonal Mansingh is a renowned Odissi dancer. She also mastered Bharatanatyam and other dance forms. She established the **Centre for Indian Classical Dances**. She has received India's highest civilian honors for her work.
Yamini Krishnamurthy excelled in Bharatanatyam & Kuchipudi. She trained under famous gurus and perfected both styles. She played a major role in popularizing classical dance globally. She received the Padma Shri, Padma Bhushan, and Padma Vibhushan.
Kalamandalam Gopi Kalamandalam Gopi is a legendary Kathakali artist known for his expressive and emotive performances. Specializing in the "Pachcha" (noble) roles, he brought characters to life with his graceful movements and deep storytelling. Trained at the renowned Kerala Kalamandalam, he became one of the most respected figures in Kathakali. His immense contribution to Indian classical dance earned him the prestigious Padma Shri award.
Indira P. P. BoraIndira P. P. Bora is a distinguished Sattriya and Bharatanatyam dancer who played a crucial role in bringing Sattriya, the classical dance form of Assam, to national recognition. With her dedication to preserving and modernizing this ancient art, she established the Kalabhumi Dance Academy, where she trains aspiring dancers. Her deep knowledge and skill have made her a revered figure in the Indian classical dance community.
Guru Bipin SinghGuru Bipin Singh was a visionary in the world of Manipuri dance, celebrated for his efforts in reviving and restructuring the form for modern audiences. His deep understanding of the art led him to create a structured teaching methodology, ensuring that Manipuri dance continued to flourish. He founded the Manipuri Nartanalaya, where he trained countless students, passing on the rich traditions of the dance. His lifelong dedication earned him great respect and recognition in the Indian dance world.
Kalamandalam Kalyanikutty AmmaKalamandalam Kalyanikutty Amma was a pioneering figure in Mohiniyattam, often credited with systematizing and popularizing the dance form. She worked tirelessly to restore its classical essence, emphasizing its elegance and fluidity. Her teachings and choreography helped shape Mohiniyattam into the respected classical dance it is today. Her legacy continues through her students and the institutions she helped establish.

Famous Dance Schools

KalakshetraKalakshetra is one of India's most prestigious dance institutions, founded by Rukmini Devi Arundale in 1936 in Chennai. It is dedicated to preserving the purity of Bharatanatyam, emphasizing discipline, spirituality, and artistic excellence. The school has trained generations of dancers, helping Bharatanatyam gain worldwide recognition. Its serene campus and rigorous training system make it a center of excellence for Indian classical dance.
Kathak Kendra Kathak Kendra, based in New Delhi, is the premier institution for Kathak, nurturing some of the finest dancers in the country. It was established to promote and refine the art of Kathak, offering structured training in both the Lucknow and Jaipur Gharanas. The institution also focuses on the connection between music and storytelling in Kathak performances. Many of India’s top Kathak dancers have been trained under its expert guidance..
Odissi School Odissi School represents the rich heritage of Odissi dance, deeply rooted in the temple traditions of Odisha. It focuses on the fluid movements, intricate expressions, and storytelling aspects that define the dance form. Several institutions, such as Nrityagram and Utkal Sangeet Mahavidyalaya, have played a key role in shaping Odissi into a globally respected art. These schools ensure that Odissi continues to flourish while keeping its classical essence intact..
Kalabhumi Dance Academy Sattriya School is dedicated to the promotion of Sattriya, the classical dance form of Assam, which evolved within Vaishnav monasteries. Initially performed by monks as a devotional art, it later gained recognition as a classical dance. Schools such as Kalabhumi Dance Academy and Sattriya Kendra work towards preserving its tradition while allowing for modern interpretations. Today, it is taught across India, keeping the legacy of this unique dance form alive..
Kerala Kathakali CentreKerala Kathakali Centre, located in Kochi, is one of the most respected institutions for learning and experiencing Kathakali. It offers in-depth training in the intricate facial expressions, elaborate hand gestures, and powerful storytelling that define this classical dance. The center also promotes other traditional art forms, making it a hub for cultural learning. Its performances and workshops attract students and art lovers from around the world.
Kalamandalam is Kerala’s premier institution for classical performing artsKalamandalam is Kerala’s premier institution for classical performing arts, specializing in Kathakali, Mohiniyattam, Koodiyattam, and other traditional forms. Established by Vallathol Narayana Menon in 1930, it has been instrumental in preserving Kerala’s artistic heritage. The school follows the traditional Gurukul system, offering rigorous training to students. It has produced many of the finest artists in India’s classical dance and theater scene.
Kuchipudi Art AcademyKuchipudi Art Academy, founded by the legendary dancer Vempati Chinna Satyam, has played a crucial role in shaping the modern form of Kuchipudi. The academy emphasizes the balance between graceful movements and dramatic storytelling, a hallmark of this vibrant dance. Through structured training and innovative choreography, it has helped Kuchipudi gain international recognition. Many of its students have become leading performers and teachers of the art.
The Jawaharlal Nehru Manipur Dance Academy in ImphalThe Jawaharlal Nehru Manipur Dance Academy in Imphal is the foremost institution for Manipuri dance, preserving and promoting this delicate and spiritual art form. Established in 1954, it offers rigorous training in Manipuri dance, which is known for its soft, flowing movements and deep connection to mythology. The academy has been a center for research, documentation, and performance, ensuring that the dance remains a vital part of India's cultural heritage. Many of its graduates have become renowned Manipuri artists.
